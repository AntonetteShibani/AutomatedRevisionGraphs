Critical thinking has become an important area of research in the health domain due to future health professionals need to develop this core skill in order to succeed in their daily clinical work. Substantial evidence from the cognitive sciences suggest that individuals who learn to address ill-structured tasks may be able to develop critical thinking skills. Kaplan (2009), has been previously proposed that individuals develop better critical thinking skills by generating self-thinking individually. However, limited research has been conducted to establish whether critical thinking could be enhanced by working collaboratively. The thinking aloud teaching strategy allows students to construct meaningful knowledge from their learning activity. The purpose of this research is to explore how students can develop critical skills working in groups using a thinking aloud teaching strategy.
